Kangaroo

The word kangaroo derives from the Guugu Yimithirr word gangurru, referring to eastern grey kangaroos.The name was first recorded as "kanguru" on 12 July 1770

  • Scientific Name: Macropus rufus
  • Average Length: 1.6 meter
  • Average Lifespan: 7 years
  • Habitat: Australia

Macropus rufus, or the red kangaroo, is the largest marsupial native to Australia. It has reddish-brown fur, powerful hind legs for hopping, and is well-adapted to arid environments. Red kangaroos feed on grasses, form social groups called mobs, and are active during cooler parts of the day. They play a crucial role in their ecosystem.
